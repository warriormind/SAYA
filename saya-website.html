Why SAYA?

Sustainable agriculture is essential in addressing global and local challenges such as climate change, food insecurity, unemployment, and poverty. Young people represent the energy, creativity, and innovation needed to transform the agricultural landscape.

SAYA exists to:

  • Equip farmers with knowledge and modern skills
  • Create strong and reliable market networks
  • Build confidence in local farming communities
  • Advocate for policy reforms and youth inclusion
  • Introduce technology to accelerate agricultural growth

SAYA is a movement, a platform, and a collective voice working toward a sustainable, innovative, and food-secure Zambia.

Our Structure

Governance

Transparent leadership and democratic decision-making

Governance Structures

General Assembly

The highest decision-making body, held annually with all members.

Executive Committee

Responsible for strategic leadership and day-to-day operations.

Sub-Committees

Specialized committees for Technology, Finance, Membership, Aquaculture, Livestock, Markets, and more.

Leadership Roles

Chairperson

Leads SAYA, oversees operations, and represents the movement nationally and internationally

Vice-Chairperson

Assists the Chairperson and assumes duties in their absence

Secretary

Maintains documentation, communication, meeting minutes, and official records

Treasurer

Manages finances, reporting, budgeting, and financial transparency

Sub-Committee Heads

Lead specific departments and initiatives

Elections & Terms

  • Leadership elections held every two years
  • Executive members serve a two-year renewable term, maximum two terms
  • All decisions made by majority vote or consensus
  • General Assembly meets annually
  • Executive Committee meets quarterly or as needed

Financial Management

Revenue Sources:

  • Membership fees
  • Donations and grants
  • Strategic partnerships
  • Income-generating activities (seedlings, trainings, produce sales)

The Treasurer ensures transparent financial management with full Executive Committee oversight and regular reporting to the General Assembly.
